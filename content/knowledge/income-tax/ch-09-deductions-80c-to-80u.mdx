## Key Takeaways

- **Chapter VI-A deductions** (Sections 80C to 80U) reduce taxable income and are available primarily under the **old tax regime**.
- **Section 80C** offers up to Rs. 1,50,000 deduction for investments in PPF, ELSS, life insurance, home loan principal, etc.
- Under the **new tax regime**, only **Section 80CCD(2)** (employer's NPS contribution) and **Section 80JJAA** (new employment) are available.
- **Section 80D** (health insurance) allows up to Rs. 1,00,000 deduction for self, family, and parents.
- Total deductions under Chapter VI-A cannot exceed the Gross Total Income.

<div className="amendment-banner">
<strong>Budget 2025-26 Update:</strong> Under the new tax regime (default from AY 2025-26), the employer's NPS contribution limit under Section 80CCD(2) has been enhanced to 14% of salary for all employers (previously 14% only for Central Government, 10% for others). Most other Chapter VI-A deductions remain unavailable under the new regime. Under the old regime, all deductions continue without change.
</div>

## Section 80C - The Primary Tax Saver (Limit: Rs. 1,50,000)

Section 80C provides deduction up to Rs. 1,50,000 for investments and expenditures specified therein. Available to Individuals and HUFs.

| Investment/Expenditure | Key Details |
|----------------------|-------------|
| Public Provident Fund (PPF) | Max Rs. 1,50,000/year; 15-year lock-in; EEE status |
| Employee Provident Fund (EPF) | Employee's contribution (12% of basic + DA) |
| ELSS Mutual Funds | Shortest lock-in among 80C options (3 years) |
| Life Insurance Premium | Max 10% of sum assured (policies after 01.04.2012) |
| NSC (National Savings Certificate) | 5-year lock-in; accrued interest reinvested also qualifies |
| 5-Year Tax Saver FD | With scheduled bank or post office |
| Sukanya Samriddhi Account | For girl child; EEE status |
| Home Loan Principal Repayment | Including stamp duty and registration charges |
| Tuition Fees | For up to 2 children; full-time education in India |
| Senior Citizen Savings Scheme | For persons aged 60+ |

**Combined limit:** Sections 80C + 80CCC (pension fund) + 80CCD(1) (self NPS contribution) together cannot exceed Rs. 1,50,000.

## Section 80CCD - National Pension System

| Sub-Section | Deduction For | Limit | Regime Availability |
|-------------|--------------|-------|-------------------|
| 80CCD(1) | Self-contribution to NPS | Included in 80C limit of Rs. 1,50,000 (max 10% of salary for employed / 20% of GTI for others) | Old regime only |
| 80CCD(1B) | Additional self-contribution to NPS | Rs. 50,000 over and above 80C limit | Old regime only |
| 80CCD(2) | Employer's contribution to NPS | 14% of salary (Basic + DA) | **Both old and new regime** |

This makes employer NPS contribution the most tax-efficient benefit under the new regime.

## Section 80D - Health Insurance Premium

| Category | Deduction Limit |
|----------|----------------|
| Self, spouse, dependent children (below 60) | Rs. 25,000 |
| Self, spouse, dependent children (any member is 60+) | Rs. 50,000 |
| Parents (below 60) | Rs. 25,000 |
| Parents (60 or above) | Rs. 50,000 |
| **Maximum total deduction** | **Rs. 1,00,000** (if self and parents are all senior citizens) |

Includes: Medical insurance premium + preventive health check-up (up to Rs. 5,000 within overall limit) + medical expenditure for senior citizens without insurance.

**Payment must be made by non-cash mode** (cheque/online) except for preventive health check-up.

## Section 80G - Donations

### 100% Deduction Without Limit
- National Defence Fund, PM National Relief Fund, National Foundation for Communal Harmony, Zila Sainik Board
- PM CARES Fund

### 50% Deduction Without Limit
- Jawaharlal Nehru Memorial Fund, PM Drought Relief Fund

### 100% Deduction With 10% of Adjusted GTI Limit
- Donations to approved scientific research associations, rural development programmes

### 50% Deduction With 10% of Adjusted GTI Limit
- Any approved charitable institution, temple, church, gurudwara (with 80G certificate)
- Cash donations exceeding Rs. 2,000 are NOT eligible

## Landmark Judgement

<div className="judgement-box">

**Case:** CIT v. Rajesh Kumar Jalan

**Court:** Calcutta High Court

**Year:** 2006

**Ruling:** The High Court held that Section 80C deduction for tuition fees paid for children's education is available for fees paid to any university, college, school, or educational institution in India. The term "tuition fees" includes admission fees and term fees but excludes development fees, donation, transport charges, or hostel charges. The court clarified that the deduction is restricted to the tuition fee component only.

**Impact:** This ruling provides clarity on what constitutes "tuition fees" under Section 80C. Taxpayers should carefully segregate the fee components in the school/college receipt and claim only the tuition fee portion. Development fees, building fund contributions, and other charges do not qualify for deduction under Section 80C.

</div>

## Worked Example - Maximising Deductions (Old Regime)

**Mr. Anil** (age 45, salaried, old tax regime) wants to maximise his tax savings. His GTI is Rs. 18,00,000.

| Deduction | Investment/Expense | Amount (Rs.) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **80C** | EPF (employee share) | 72,000 |
| 80C | PPF contribution | 50,000 |
| 80C | ELSS mutual fund | 20,000 |
| 80C | Tuition fees (2 children) | 40,000 |
| 80C | Home loan principal | 30,000 |
| **80C Total** (capped at 1,50,000) | | **1,50,000** |
| **80CCD(1B)** | Additional NPS contribution | **50,000** |
| **80CCD(2)** | Employer NPS (10% of Basic 6L + DA 1.2L) | **72,000** |
| **80D** | Health insurance (self + family) | 25,000 |
| 80D | Health insurance (parents, 65 yrs) | 50,000 |
| **80D Total** | | **75,000** |
| **80E** | Education loan interest (son's MBA) | **1,20,000** |
| **80TTA** | Savings account interest | **10,000** |
| **Total Chapter VI-A Deductions** | | **4,77,000** |

**Taxable Income** = 18,00,000 - 4,77,000 = **Rs. 13,23,000**

This saves approximately Rs. 1,43,100 in tax (at 30% slab) compared to no deductions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Can I claim both Section 80C and Section 24(b) for a home loan?**

Yes. Section 80C allows deduction for the **principal repayment** component of home loan EMI (up to Rs. 1,50,000). Section 24(b) allows deduction for the **interest component** (up to Rs. 2,00,000 for self-occupied property). These are separate deductions under different sections and can be claimed simultaneously.

**Q2: Is Section 80G deduction available under the new tax regime?**

No. Section 80G deduction for donations is NOT available under the new tax regime (Section 115BAC). If you make substantial charitable donations, evaluate whether the old regime provides better overall tax savings. The only Chapter VI-A deductions available under the new regime are 80CCD(2) and 80JJAA.

**Q3: Can I invest in PPF and ELSS in my spouse's name and claim 80C deduction?**

For PPF, yes - you can contribute to your spouse's PPF account and claim 80C deduction (but the contribution is counted within your Rs. 1,50,000 limit). For ELSS, the investment must be in your own name to claim the deduction - you cannot claim 80C for ELSS units purchased in your spouse's name.
