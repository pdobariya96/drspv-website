## Key Takeaways

- **TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)** is a mechanism to collect tax at the point of income generation - the payer deducts and deposits tax on behalf of the payee.
- **TCS (Tax Collected at Source)** is collected by the seller from the buyer on specified transactions (LRS, cars, scrap, minerals, etc.).
- **Advance tax** must be paid in **4 instalments** if total tax liability exceeds Rs. 10,000 in a year.
- **Section 234A/234B/234C** impose interest for delays in filing, shortfall in advance tax, and deferment of instalments respectively.
- All TDS/TCS details are reflected in **Form 26AS** and **Annual Information Statement (AIS)** - verify before filing returns.

## Advance Tax - Instalments and Due Dates

Every person whose estimated tax liability for the year (after TDS) exceeds **Rs. 10,000** must pay advance tax.

**Exception:** Resident senior citizens (60+) with NO income from business/profession are exempt from advance tax.

| Instalment | Due Date | Cumulative % of Tax |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| 1st | 15th June | 15% |
| 2nd | 15th September | 45% |
| 3rd | 15th December | 75% |
| 4th | 15th March | 100% |

**Presumptive taxation (44AD/44ADA):** Entire advance tax to be paid in one instalment by **15th March**.

## Interest and Penalties

| Section | Nature | Rate | Period |
|---------|--------|------|--------|
| 234A | Delay in filing return | 1% per month (part month = full month) | From due date to filing date, on tax due |
| 234B | Short payment of advance tax (< 90% of assessed tax) | 1% per month | April to date of assessment |
| 234C | Deferment of advance tax instalment | 1% per month (3 months per instalment) | For shortfall in each instalment |
| 201(1A) | Late deposit of TDS | 1% per month (non-deduction) / 1.5% per month (deducted but not deposited) | From due date to deposit date |
| 271C | Penalty for non-deduction of TDS | Amount equal to TDS not deducted | Levied by JCIT |
| 234E | Late filing of TDS return | Rs. 200/day | Until the return is filed (max = TDS amount) |

## Landmark Judgement

<div className="judgement-box">

**Case:** Hindustan Coca-Cola Beverages Pvt. Ltd. v. CIT

**Court:** Supreme Court of India

**Year:** 2007

**Ruling:** The Supreme Court held that when the payee (recipient of income) has paid taxes on the income received, the payer (deductor) cannot be treated as an assessee-in-default under Section 201(1) for non-deduction of TDS. However, the payer remains liable for interest under Section 201(1A) for the period of delay. The deductor must demonstrate that the payee has included the income in their return and paid tax thereon.

**Impact:** This ruling provides significant relief to deductors who inadvertently fail to deduct TDS. While the TDS demand itself can be quashed if the payee has paid tax, interest liability remains. Taxpayers should obtain certificates or proof of tax payment from payees in such situations to substantiate their defence before the Assessing Officer.

</div>

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Can I claim TDS credit if the deductor has not deposited the TDS with the government?**

Yes. Under Section 199 read with Rule 37BA, you can claim TDS credit based on the TDS certificate (Form 16/16A) issued by the deductor. However, the credit may initially be denied during processing if 26AS does not reflect the entry. In such cases, file a grievance and follow up with the deductor to deposit the TDS. The Supreme Court in Hindustan Coca-Cola Beverages has clarified that the payee should not suffer for the deductor's default.

**Q2: Is TCS adjustable against my income tax liability?**

Yes. TCS paid or collected is treated as advance tax and is available as credit against your total tax liability for the year. It is reflected in your Form 26AS. You can claim the credit while filing your income tax return, similar to TDS credit. Ensure the TCS is correctly reflecting under your PAN in the AIS/26AS before filing.

**Q3: What happens if I miss an advance tax instalment?**

If you miss an instalment or pay less than the required cumulative percentage, interest under Section 234C is levied at 1% per month for 3 months (for the first three instalments) on the shortfall amount. For the last instalment (March), interest is for 1 month. Even if you pay the full tax later in the year, the instalment-wise shortfall still attracts 234C interest.
