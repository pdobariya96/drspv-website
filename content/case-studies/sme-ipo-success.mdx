## The Problem

A Gujarat-based technology company specialising in industrial automation software and IoT solutions had grown rapidly over 5 years — from Rs 2 crore to Rs 18 crore in annual revenue. The promoter wanted to raise Rs 10-15 crore to fund:

- A new development centre in GIFT City, Gandhinagar
- Working capital for a large government contract pipeline
- Expansion into two new states

However, the company faced several roadblocks:

- **Financial statements were under Indian GAAP** — Ind AS restatement had never been done
- **No independent directors** on the board — the entire board comprised the promoter and his family members
- **Incomplete ICFR documentation** — internal controls were informal and undocumented
- **Related-party transactions** worth Rs 1.2 crore annually with the promoter's other business — a hardware distribution company
- **Tax compliance gaps** — two years of delayed GST annual returns and an unresolved TDS demand of Rs 3.5 lakh

The promoter had approached two merchant bankers, both of whom declined to take up the mandate until the financial and governance issues were resolved. The company was referred to DRSPV by their existing statutory auditor.

## What DRSPV Did

Our CAs designed a 14-month pre-IPO preparation programme covering every aspect of IPO readiness:

**Months 1-4: Financial Clean-Up**
- Completed Ind AS restatement for 3 financial years (FY 2022-23, 2023-24, 2024-25)
- Identified and reversed Rs 14 lakh of promoter personal expenses routed through the company
- Filed pending GST annual returns (GSTR-9) for 2 years and resolved the TDS demand by filing rectification under Section 154
- Restructured the related-party transactions — converted the hardware supply arrangement to arm's-length pricing supported by a transfer pricing study

**Months 5-8: Governance & Compliance**
- Appointed 2 independent directors with relevant industry experience
- Constituted Audit Committee, Nomination & Remuneration Committee, and Stakeholder Relationship Committee
- Appointed a full-time Company Secretary
- Documented ICFR for all major business processes — revenue recognition, procurement, payroll, and financial close
- Conducted one full cycle of internal audit and ICFR testing

**Months 9-12: IPO Structuring**
- Introduced the company to a SEBI-registered merchant banker (who was now confident in the company's readiness)
- Prepared restated financial statements with audit certification
- Drafted the tax benefits statement for the offer document
- Assisted with DRHP preparation — specifically the financial information sections, objects of the issue, and capitalisation statement
- Supported the company through the BSE SME review process, addressing 4 observations from the exchange

**Months 13-14: IPO Execution**
- The IPO opened with a price band of Rs 52-55 per share
- Our CAs coordinated with the registrar on allotment and listing logistics

## The Result

| Parameter | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Platform** | BSE SME |
| **Issue Size** | Rs 12.10 crore (Fresh Issue) |
| **Price Band** | Rs 52-55 per share |
| **Subscription** | 4.8x overall (Retail: 3.2x, HNI: 7.1x) |
| **Listing Premium** | 38% (opened at Rs 76 against issue price of Rs 55) |
| **Funds Raised** | Rs 12.10 crore |
| **Total DRSPV Engagement** | 14 months |

**Post-Listing:** The company successfully set up its GIFT City development centre within 8 months of listing. Revenue grew 32% in the first full year as a listed entity. The stock was trading at approximately Rs 120 — over 2x the issue price — 12 months after listing.

**Additional Outcomes:**
- The listing enhanced the company's credibility, helping it secure 3 new government contracts worth Rs 8 crore
- The promoter's personal net worth increased by Rs 15 crore on paper
- The company was able to attract senior engineering talent by offering listed ESOPs
- Post-listing compliance was handled smoothly — no exchange penalties or delayed filings in the first year
