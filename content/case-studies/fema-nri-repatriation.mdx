## The Problem

An Indian-origin NRI residing in Dubai (UAE) for over 15 years had inherited a residential flat in Ahmedabad from his late father. The property — a 3BHK apartment purchased by his father in 2008 for Rs 32 lakh — had a current market value of approximately Rs 1.10 crore.

The NRI wanted to sell the property and **repatriate the entire sale proceeds to his UAE bank account in AED (UAE Dirhams)**. However, he was stuck due to multiple complications:

- **No clarity on capital gains tax liability** — he was unsure whether long-term capital gain (LTCG) applied, what indexation benefit was available, and whether the inheritance date or original purchase date was relevant for the holding period
- **TDS obligations** — the buyer's CA had informed the buyer that **TDS of 20% plus surcharge and cess** must be deducted under Section 195, which would block approximately Rs 22+ lakh
- **FEMA repatriation rules** — the NRI was unaware of the documentation required by his Indian bank (an NRO account with SBI) to process outward remittance under RBI's Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) and FEMA regulations
- **Form 15CA / 15CB requirements** — his bank had rejected a previous remittance attempt because the Form 15CB certificate was not furnished
- **Practical difficulties** — the NRI could not travel to India for an extended period and needed the entire matter handled remotely

A local property dealer had suggested the NRI simply transfer the money to a friend's Indian account and have it informally remitted — an approach that would have violated FEMA provisions and attracted RBI penalties.

## What DRSPV Did

Our qualified CAs took end-to-end ownership of the transaction — from sale agreement to final repatriation:

**Step 1 — Capital Gains Computation:**
- Determined that the property was inherited (Section 49(1) of the Income Tax Act) — the cost of acquisition was the original purchase price of Rs 32 lakh by the father in 2008
- Calculated the indexed cost of acquisition using CII (Cost Inflation Index):
  - CII for 2008-09: 137 | CII for 2025-26: 363
  - Indexed Cost = Rs 32,00,000 x (363/137) = **Rs 84,76,000** (approximately)
- Sale consideration: Rs 1,10,00,000
- **Long-Term Capital Gain: Rs 25,24,000** (approximately)
- **LTCG Tax at 20% + cess: Rs 5,25,000** (approximately)

**Step 2 — Lower TDS Certificate (Section 197):**
- Filed an application under **Section 197** of the Income Tax Act with the jurisdictional Assessing Officer to obtain a lower/nil TDS certificate
- Demonstrated through our computation that the actual tax liability was approximately Rs 5.25 lakh — far lower than the 20% TDS of Rs 22 lakh that would otherwise be deducted
- **Obtained a lower TDS certificate within 18 days**, directing the buyer to deduct TDS of only Rs 5,25,000 instead of Rs 22 lakh
- This freed up approximately **Rs 16.75 lakh** in cash flow for the NRI

**Step 3 — Sale Execution & Tax Compliance:**
- Coordinated with the buyer's legal counsel for sale deed execution (the NRI granted Power of Attorney to a trusted family member)
- Ensured TDS was deducted at the lower rate and deposited via Form 26QB
- Filed the NRI's Income Tax Return (ITR-2) for the relevant assessment year, claiming the LTCG and paying the balance tax

**Step 4 — FEMA Repatriation:**
- Prepared **Form 15CA (Part C)** — the online declaration required for foreign remittance
- Obtained **Form 15CB certification** — the CA certificate confirming the nature of remittance, tax compliance, and FEMA eligibility
- Compiled the complete repatriation dossier for SBI NRO account:
  - Sale deed copy
  - Income Tax Return acknowledgment
  - Form 15CA and 15CB
  - CA certificate confirming the transaction was eligible for repatriation under FEMA Section 6(5) and RBI Master Direction on remittance
  - Original purchase deed (father's) and succession certificate
- Submitted the remittance request to SBI's NRI cell
